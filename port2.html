Data, Information, and Information Systems

Data and Information

Data are raw facts and figures that on their own have little meaning, while information is data that has been processed, organized, and structured so that it becomes meaningful and useful. Data can take the form of numbers, words, measurements, or observations, and when it is analyzed and given context, it becomes information that can support decisions (IBM, 2023; TechTarget, 2021; Britannica, 2024).

Information Systems

An information system is a set of components that work together to collect, process, store, and distribute information. These components include people, hardware, software, communication networks, and data resources. Information systems are used to support decision-making, coordination, analysis, and control in organizations (Britannica, 2024; TechTarget, 2023; Syracuse iSchool, 2022).

Types of Support Systems in Information Systems

1. Transaction Processing Systems (TPS)

Computerized systems that record, process, and store routine, day-to-day business transactions such as sales, payroll, reservations, and billing. Focus on accuracy, speed, and reliability.

2. Management Information Systems (MIS)

Summarize and report on basic operations, providing structured reports and routine summaries to support short-term planning and monitoring.

3. Decision Support Systems (DSS)

Interactive software tools that analyze large amounts of data, combining models and analytical techniques to support semi-structured or unstructured decision-making.

4. Executive Support Systems (ESS)

Designed for senior executives to support strategic decision-making. Provides dashboards, summaries, and trend analyses to monitor performance.

5. Expert Systems (ES)

Computer applications that mimic human expertise using rules or a knowledge base to solve specific problems, often in areas like medical diagnosis and troubleshooting.

6. Office Automation Systems (OAS)

Technologies that support office activities such as document management, communication, scheduling, and data processing.

7. Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP)

Integrated software platforms that unify different functional areas (finance, HR, production, logistics) to improve coordination and efficiency.

8. Customer Relationship Management (CRM)

9. Supply Chain Management (SCM)

Coordinate the flow of materials, information, and finances across suppliers, manufacturers, warehouses, retailers, and customers to optimize efficiency and delivery.

Insight

Initially, I thought information systems were just about storing data or using computers for office work. Now I understand there are specific systems designed for different levels of decision-making. TPS may seem simple but is the backbone of operations. MIS provides summaries that managers use to monitor performance. DSS and ESS support complex analysis and strategic decision-making. Overall, these systems are interconnected and together make an organization run effectively. As a future IT professional, understanding these systems motivates me to see technology as real solutions that impact businesses and society.
