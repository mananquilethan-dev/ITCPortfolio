Comparative Study on Different Types of Motherboards

Introduction

Motherboards serve as the backbone of a computer system, connecting all essential components such as the CPU, memory, storage, and peripheral devices. Various motherboard form factors exist to suit different computing needs, from large-scale servers to compact home systems. Understanding their differences helps in identifying the right type for specific applications.

Discussion

The evolution of motherboard designs has been driven by the demand for improved performance, compactness, and better heat management. Early designs like AT and LPX were bulky and limited in expandability, while modern ones like ATX and Mini ITX offer efficient layouts, faster data transfer, and more built-in features. Each motherboard type varies in size, number of slots, and supported hardware, making some ideal for gaming, others for servers, and some for embedded systems.
